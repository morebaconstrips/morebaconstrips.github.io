Whistle Blower

While going through some books, Alice found the identity of the whistleblower in their company and was utterly suprised by the name. She needed to inform Eve about it but she didn’t know who is watching her emails. So she decided to hide the message in her email to Eve. See if you can figure out who betrayed the company.

Download: Dear_Eve.txt

Content of the file:

Dear Eve,

Thank you for your email informing me of your absence. I hope you feel better soon. Do not worry about your presentation with the Stegno Space Ltd. I can cover for you. Could you please send me your notes for that.

Thank you, 				 		
Best, 	    	
Alice

After analyzing the file and noticing suspicious spaces and tabs at the end of the mail I thought that the secret message could have been hidden there.

So I mapped every tab and space in 1s and 0s and decoded them from binary with space as delimiter and 7 as byte length. here’s what I got:

input: 1100010 1011111 0110001 1110011 1011111 0110001 1001000 0110011 1011111 1110111 0110001 1110011 0110111 1001100 0110011 1011111 1000010 1101100 0110000 1110111 0110011 1110010 1111101
output: b_1s_1H3_w1s7L3_Bl0w3r}

It looks like a piece of flag!

I did the same mapping with the tabs and spaces at the end of each line from the beginning, although I had to change the byte length to 8.

input: 00000001 01010100 01001000 01010011 01111011 01000010 00110000

output: THS{B0

Then I retrieved the flag by simply concatenating the strings

THS{B0b_1s_1H3_w1s7L3_Bl0w3r}
